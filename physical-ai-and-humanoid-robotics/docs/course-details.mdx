## Course Overview

Welcome to "Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics," an advanced course exploring the intersection of artificial intelligence and robotics. This course provides a comprehensive understanding of the principles, technologies, and applications involved in developing intelligent humanoid robots capable of interacting with the physical world. Students will delve into topics ranging from robot operating systems and simulation environments to advanced AI models for perception, language understanding, and action generation. The curriculum emphasizes hands-on learning, practical implementation, and a strong theoretical foundation.

## Learning Objectives

Upon successful completion of this course, students will be able to:

*   Understand the architecture and components of modern robotic systems.
*   Utilize ROS 2 for robot communication, control, and sensor integration.
*   Develop and test robot behaviors in realistic simulation environments like Gazebo and Unity.
*   Apply NVIDIA Isaac tools and platforms for AI-powered robot perception and navigation.
*   Explore Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models for advanced robot intelligence and human interaction.
*   Design and implement autonomous tasks for humanoid robots.
*   Evaluate ethical considerations and societal impact of advanced robotics.

## Target Audience

This course is designed for graduate students, researchers, and professionals with a background in robotics, artificial intelligence, computer science, or a related engineering discipline. Familiarity with programming (Python/C++) and basic concepts of AI and control systems is recommended.

## Prerequisites

*   Proficiency in Python and/or C++.
*   Foundational knowledge in linear algebra and calculus.
*   Basic understanding of artificial intelligence concepts (e.g., machine learning, neural networks).
*   Experience with Linux command line interfaces.
