## Quarter Overview (13 Weeks)

This course is structured over a 13-week quarter, combining lectures, practical labs, and project-based learning to provide a comprehensive experience in Physical AI and Humanoid Robotics. Each week builds upon previous concepts, culminating in a capstone project.

### Week-by-Week Schedule

*   **Week 1: Introduction to Physical AI and ROS 2 Fundamentals**
    *   Overview of physical AI, embodied intelligence, and humanoid robotics.
    *   ROS 2 architecture, nodes, topics, services, and actions.
    *   Lab: Setting up ROS 2 workspace and basic command line tools.

*   **Week 2: ROS 2 Advanced Concepts and Ecosystem**
    *   ROS 2 launch files, parameters, and tf2 (coordinate transformations).
    *   Introduction to RViz and rqt tools for visualization and debugging.
    *   Lab: Implementing basic robot control with ROS 2 (publishers/subscribers).

*   **Week 3: Robotic Simulation with Gazebo**
    *   Introduction to Gazebo, world creation, and robot models (URDF/SDF).
    *   Integrating ROS 2 with Gazebo for simulated robots.
    *   Lab: Simulating a simple robot in Gazebo and controlling it via ROS 2.

*   **Week 4: High-Fidelity Simulation with Unity**
    *   Overview of Unity for robotics, asset integration, and scene setup.
    *   Connecting Unity simulations to ROS 2 (ROS-TCP-Connector).
    *   Lab: Creating a custom robot scene in Unity and basic ROS 2 control.

*   **Week 5: NVIDIA Isaac SDK: Perception Fundamentals**
    *   Introduction to Isaac SDK, its components, and deployment.
    *   Camera processing, object detection, and segmentation using Isaac.
    *   Lab: Implementing AI perception tasks in Isaac Sim.

*   **Week 6: NVIDIA Isaac SDK: Navigation and Localization**
    *   SLAM (Simultaneous Localization and Mapping) concepts.
    *   Path planning, obstacle avoidance, and navigation stacks in Isaac.
    *   Lab: Autonomous navigation in a simulated environment using Isaac SDK.

*   **Week 7: NVIDIA Isaac SDK: Manipulation**
    *   Robot kinematics (forward and inverse) and motion planning for robotic arms.
    *   Grasping techniques and manipulation primitives.
    *   Lab: Object manipulation tasks in Isaac Sim.

*   **Week 8: Vision-Language-Action (VLA) Models: Theory**
    *   Foundations of multimodal AI, language grounding, and embodied cognition.
    *   Architectures for integrating vision and language for robotic control.
    *   Discussion: Challenges and opportunities in VLA research.

*   **Week 9: VLA Models: Practical Implementation**
    *   Techniques for training and deploying VLA models for robot tasks.
    *   Frameworks and tools for connecting language commands to robot actions.
    *   Lab: Implementing a simple VLA-driven task in simulation.

*   **Week 10: Capstone Project: Design and Planning**
    *   Project proposal and team formation (if applicable).
    *   Detailed design of the autonomous humanoid system.
    *   Setting up development environment and initial component integration.

*   **Week 11: Capstone Project: Implementation Phase 1**
    *   Development of core robot functionalities.
    *   Integration of ROS 2, simulation, and basic AI modules.
    *   Mid-project review and troubleshooting.

*   **Week 12: Capstone Project: Implementation Phase 2 and Testing**
    *   Refinement of AI algorithms and robot behaviors.
    *   Extensive testing, debugging, and performance optimization.
    *   Lab: Finalizing the autonomous humanoid system.

*   **Week 13: Capstone Project: Presentation and Future Directions**
    *   Project demonstrations and presentations.
    *   Discussion on future work, scalability, and societal impact of humanoid robotics.
    *   Course wrap-up and Q&A.

### Conclusion

This weekly breakdown provides a roadmap for a rigorous and engaging learning experience, guiding students through the theoretical underpinnings and practical applications of Physical AI and Humanoid Robotics.
